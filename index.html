    US History

    To be ignorant of the past is to remain a child. ~Cicero

You cannot understand the present without a valid understanding of the past. ~Mr. K

    The course is a chronological survey of the events and forces that shaped our nation. Although the study of history can be interesting on its own, one of the greatest benefits of studying history is to better understand how the issues of today are shaped by historical events. Consequently, relating current events to historical events will be an important part of this class. In order to give you a broad understanding of US and AZ history, we will be studying the following units this year:

    First Semester

    Unit 1. American Beginnings to 1783

    Unit 2. A New Nation (1781-1850)

    Unit 3. An Era of Growth & Disunion (1825-1877)

    Unit 4. Migration & Industrialization (1877-1917)

    Unit 5. Modern America Emerges (1890-1920)

    Second Semester

    Unit 6. The 1920s & the Great Depression (1919-1940)

    Unit 7. World War II & Its Aftermath (1931-1960)

    Unit 8. Living with Great Turmoil (1954-1975)

    Unit 9. Passage to a New Century (1968-2001)

    Unit 10. The 21st century (2001-?)
